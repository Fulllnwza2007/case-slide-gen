# Shell Spec

Use one fixed shell family across all non-cover slides.

## Canvas

- exact `16:9` widescreen presentation slide canvas
- full-slide composition, not a poster crop or square board
- landscape slide framing from edge to edge

This skill targets presentation slides, not posters, cards, or social-media graphics.

## Headline Area

- one fixed-height headline zone at the top
- one short row-1 section/title line only
- one dominant row-2 takeaway line only
- one short row-3 supporting subdetail line only
- one clean divider treatment directly below the headline zone
- no vertical left accent bar

## Headline Proportion Rules

The headline area is not a loose family. It should follow this pattern:

- the headline zone is visually large and stable across non-cover slides
- row 1 is compact, subordinate, and clearly shorter than row 2
- row 2 is the main statement and visually dominant within the headline zone
- row 3 is a short supporting subdetail line beneath the main statement
- rows 2 and 3 should remain contained within the fixed headline zone
- the body must begin below the divider and must not float upward into the headline area
- if the row-2 takeaway is too long, rewrite it shorter instead of wrapping into extra rows
- do not solve overflow by shrinking the shell structure itself

Do not move these elements into side rails, stacked title cards, or poster-style overlays.

## Bottom Flow Spec

The bottom flow is not a generic strip. It should follow this family:

- one thin horizontal rule running across the top edge of the flow area
- section labels arranged on a single centered horizontal line
- one active section marked by a short, thick underline or top marker directly above the active label
- labels remain typographic, not boxed tabs, pills, badges, or side navigation
- brand mark or deck owner mark sits at the far right edge of the flow area
- the flow area remains slim and understated, not a heavy footer bar

## Non-Negotiables

- do not render in any ratio other than exact `16:9`
- do not crop the composition into a poster, square board, portrait card, or floating panel
- do not add extra headline rows beyond the fixed three-row structure
- do not use a vertical left accent bar
- do not let body content rise into the headline zone
- do not solve long takeaways by shrinking the shell structure
- do not turn the bottom flow into top tabs
- do not replace the active marker with filled pills or large blocks
- do not scatter labels across the page width unevenly
- do not remove the far-right brand mark
- do not let the footer become a decorative banner
